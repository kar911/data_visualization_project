OVERALL - ANALYSIS








⇨  Now we know how these factors affect the GSDP and GSDP per capita of our nation. Let's take a look at      something more fundamental, agriculture.

⇨  India is known as an agricultural country all over the world, it can be called the core block of our nation.
⇨  In a country where over 50 percent of the population is involved in agriculture or is involved in things related to      agriculture.


How much does this sector affect our GDP?

To find this out, we will find the correlation between the GSDP and some of the most prominent agricultural factors.

The factors are :

  • How much agricultural and non-agricultural land a state has.
  • How much of it is irrigated.
  • How much of it is used in agriculture





Heatmap for Agricultural Factors


{% include './graphs/Heatmap for Agricultural Factors.html' %}


Conclusion
  • While the agricultural factors have a positive impact on total GSDP, it has a negative impact on GSDP per capita.




⇨  The correlation shows the impact agriculture has on GSDP, lets take a look at how much does it contribute to      our economy.
⇨  Below is a pie chart of the contribution of sectors to the GDP of India.


{% include './graphs/PieChart - Contribution of Sectors in GSDP.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The largest contributor to GDP is the tertiary sector with a contribution of 48 percent, followed by the secondary sector with a contribution of 23.4 percent, the primary sector contributing the least with a contribution of 18.9 percent.
  • The "Others" category includes the taxes paid to the Government in the form of Income Tax Returns and GST/Tax on products.
  • The Pie chart is based on the GDP of the year 2020.




⇨  Now, let's find out what this contribution standing was in the past.
⇨  For that, we will take a look at a line chart representing the growth in GDP contribution by the sectors over 10      years.


{% include './graphs/GDP Distribution of India over the Years.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The exponential growth in the contribution of the tertiary sector is quite clear to see, while there is continuous growth in the other sectors too, it has been dwarfed by the growth of the tertiary sector.




⇨  Till now, we have been analyzing how much the sectors contribute to India as a whole, so let's take a look at the      state-wise sector contribution.
⇨  For this, we will compare the states by Total GSDP and GSDP per capita and see how much the contribution      each sector has in their GSDP.



{% include './graphs/Comparision of States by Sector Distribution.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The states are arranged based on their total GSDP, the chart includes the top 5 states by both GSDP and GSDP per capita.
  • The trend which can be seen in this chart is that the states with a higher GSDP per capita tend to have a higher secondary sector contribution while the states with a lower GSDP per capita tend to have higher primary sector contribution.

How does Different Factors affect each other








  • Relation between Literacy and Human Development Index
  • Effect of Literacy Rate on Birthrate

{% include './graphs/Relation between Literacy and Human Development Index.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The Correlation Value between Literacy Rate and Human Development Index is 0.77.
  • A very clear trend that can be seen here is that the Human Development Index and Literacy Rate are directly proportional to each other.
  • With both of them having a clear positive impact on the each other.

⇨  As we have seen above, HDI and Literacy Rate are directly proportional to each other, so for the next part, we      will skip HDI and observe the relationship of just Literacy Rate with other factors.


{% include './graphs/Effect of Literacy on Birthrate.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The Correlation Value between Literacy Rate and Birthrate is -0.58.
  • The trend that can be observed in this chart is that as the Literacy Rate of a state grows, the birthrate of the said state drops.
  • This shows that the birth rate is inversely proportional to the literacy rate.
  • The same is true for the Human Development Index.
  • The Ols trend line signifies the '-0.58' correlation.

Conclusion

  • The trend that can be observed in this chart is that as the Literacy Rate of a state grows, the Infant Mortality Rate of the said state drops.
  • This shows that the Infant Mortality Rate is inversely proportional to the literacy rate.
  • The same is true for the Human Development Index.
  • The Ols trend line signifies the '-0.74' correlation.

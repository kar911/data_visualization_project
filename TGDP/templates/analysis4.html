OVERALL - ANALYSIS








⇨  Till now, we have seen how the different factors affect our GDP, the contribution of different sectors to our GDP,      and how these different factors affect each other. The thing that is common in all of them is that they're all from      an economic point of view.

⇨  Let's take a philosophical approach as well.

⇨  There's a saying we've all heard of "Money can't buy Happiness", let's see how true is this saying.





To find this out, let's take a look at a heatmap showing the correlation between the happiness index and some factors.


{% include './graphs/How does these Factors affect the Happiness Index.html' %}


Conclusion
  • There's no conclusive enough correlation between the happiness index and other factors.
  • While Human Development Index and literacy rate has a positive impact, it's negligible.




While this heatmap in itself is enough for us to conclude. Let's take a look at the rankings of the state by their happiness rating.



{% include './graphs/Ranking of States by Happiness Rating.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The state having the highest happiness rating are 'Mizoram' 'Punjab' 'Andaman and Nicobar' 'Puducherry' 'Sikkim'.
  • And the state having the lowest happiness rating are 'Chattisgarh' 'Uttarkhand' 'Goa' 'Meghalaya' 'Orissa'.
  • Madhya Pradesh is the 10th from the Bottom.




⇨ The ranking clearly shows it all. Happiness can't be based on a single factor or even a group of them.

⇨  It's a thing that doesn't have a concrete formula.

⇨  Mizoram a state which ranks in the bottom part of every other ranking whether it's GSDP or GSDP per capita      being the happiest smashes everyone who says "Money is happiness" right in the face.

⇨  Goa has the highest GSDP per capita, but it has one of the lowest happiness ratings.

⇨  Uttar Pradesh has the lowest GSDP per capita, it has the lowest Human Development Index, it has one of the       lowest literacy rates, but it has achieved a spot in the top 10 happiest states.



All these stats prooves the saying "Money can't buy Happiness"

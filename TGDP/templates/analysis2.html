Relation between the Human Development Index and the GDP per Capita



What is the Human Development Index?

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a summary measure of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable and have a decent standard of living. The HDI is the geometric mean of normalized indices for each of the three dimensions.




{% include './graphs/Ranking of States by Human Development Index.html' %}



{% include './graphs/Relation between Human Development Index and GSDP per Capit.html' %}


Conclusion
  • The Correlation Value between Human Development Index and GSDP per Capita is 0.74
  • The chart shows that HDI and GSDP per capita are directly proportional to each other to a degree.
  • Goa has ranked the highest while Bihar has ranked the lowest in terms of Human Development Index
  • The Ols trend line signifies the '0.74' correlation.
  • The exceptions are the result of different factors affecting the GSDP per Capita
